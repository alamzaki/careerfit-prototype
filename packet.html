Executive Summary

The problem (student side): Many students feel stuck choosing a path. There’s too much generic advice and it’s hard to see real ROI.

The problem (university side): Career teams must show outcomes, not just “skills taught,” and need clearer signals about where students fit.

My idea: a lightweight tool that gives students 3–5 market-aware career matches in minutes, then nudges them into simple, concrete next steps.

How I’m testing it: lead with one hook — a short quiz. If students complete it and request their full report, that’s our demand signal.

Concept Map

Target Users

Primary: students (18–26) who are ambitious but overwhelmed — they’ve tried courses and advice, but still can’t pick a path.

Secondary: university career services that need to show real career outcomes and improve placement satisfaction.

Value Proposition

  • For students: quick clarity (3–5 best-fit options) matched to strengths and real demand — plus a simple next step.
  • For universities: shift from “we teach skills” to “we guide careers,” with better reporting on outcomes.

Five Big Features

  1. Career Fit Quiz (Hook): 6 questions → instant 3–5 options.
  2. Market Demand Dashboard: salary bands, demand level, growth outlook.
  3. Recruiting Tracker: one place for applications, interviews, referrals.
  4. Peer Benchmarking: anonymous “people like you also explored…” insights.
  5. Action Roadmap: micro-skills and starter projects for each path.

Experiment Build

I built the smallest possible surface to read demand quickly. If students find value, they’ll ask for more; if not, we learn and adjust fast.

What’s in the Prototype

  • Landing Page: one clear promise (“Find your career fit in under 2 minutes”) and a single CTA.
  • Career Fit Quiz: 6 short questions about what matters, strengths, and work style.
  • Instant Suggestions: 3 paths shown immediately; email unlock for the full report (salary, demand, next steps).
  • Quick Feedback: thumbs-up/down on result “feel.”

Metrics & Decisions

  • Primary: Quiz → Email conversion (demand).
  • Secondary: thumbs-up rate (accuracy feel) and school interest (viability).

Decision Rules

  • Positive: ≥ 25% conversion → build MVP-1 (market dashboard + recruiting tracker), run a small pilot with 1–2 departments.
  • Negative: < 12% or low thumbs-up → try a different hook (e.g., lead with Recruiting Tracker), refine copy/quiz, re-test.
